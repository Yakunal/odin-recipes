Description

Pizza is a popular Italian dish that has become a global favorite, known for its delicious combination of a crisp, chewy crust topped with flavorful ingredients. It typically starts with a round, flat base of dough made from flour, yeast, water, and olive oil. The dough is spread with a layer of tomato sauce, then topped with cheese—most commonly mozzarella—and a variety of toppings such as pepperoni, vegetables, mushrooms, olives, or meats. The pizza is then baked in a hot oven until the crust is golden and the cheese is melted and bubbly. With endless topping combinations and styles—from thin and crispy Neapolitan to deep-dish Chicago-style—pizza is incredibly versatile and can be enjoyed by nearly anyone, making it a beloved comfort food around the world.

Ingredients

  • Pre made pizza base
  • Pizza sauce
  • Toppings premix
  • Shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Spices and seasoning
  • Oregano and chiili flakes
  • Olive oil

Steps

  1. Preheat Your Oven or Pan

    Oven: Preheat to 220 degree celsius for about 10 minutes.

    Pan: Heat a thick-bottomed pan with a lid on low flame (no oil needed yet).
  2. Place the ready-made pizza base on a tray or plate. Spread 3–4 tablespoons of pizza sauce evenly over the base.
  3. Sprinkle your topping premix (vegetables or meat) evenly over the sauce. Make sure to drain any excess water or oil from the topping mix if needed.
  4. Add Cheese: Generously cover with shredded mozzarella cheese. Optionally, sprinkle some oregano and chili flakes.

  5. Bake or Cook:

    In Oven: Place the pizza on a baking tray or pizza stone. Bake for 8–12 minutes, or until cheese is melted and bubbly and the crust is slightly golden.

    In Pan (without oven): Place the pizza in the preheated pan. Cover with lid. Cook on low flame for 10–12 minutes, or until cheese melts and bottom is crisp.
  6. Finish & Serve: Optionally drizzle a bit of olive oil on top. Slice and serve hot with extra oregano and chili flakes.
