Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish known for its rich, layered structure and comforting flavors. It consists of wide, flat pasta sheets alternated with layers of savory meat sauce—typically made from ground beef or pork simmered with tomatoes, onions, garlic, and herbs—and a creamy ricotta cheese mixture, often blended with egg and Parmesan. Each layer is topped with shredded mozzarella, creating a gooey, golden crust when baked. The dish is assembled in a deep pan and baked until bubbling and golden brown, allowing the flavors to meld together beautifully. Lasagna is hearty, satisfying, and often enjoyed as a main course during family gatherings or special occasions. It can also be customized with vegetables, white sauce (béchamel), or even made vegetarian, making it a versatile and beloved comfort food around the world.

Ingredients

  • Meat
  • Onion and garlic
  • Tomato products
  • Sugar
  • Spices and seasoning
  • Lasagna noodles
  • Cheeses
  • Egg

Steps

  1. Tomato products: You'll need a can of crushed tomatoes, two cans of tomato sauce, and two cans of tomato paste.
  2. Sugar: Two tablespoons of white sugar add subtle sweetness and enhance the flavor of the sauce.
  3. Spices and seasonings: This lasagna recipe is flavored with fresh parsley, dried basil leaves, salt, Italian seasoning, fennel seeds, and black pepper.
  4. Lasagna noodles: Use store-bought or homemade lasagna noodles.
  5. Cheeses: Parmesan, mozzarella, and ricotta cheese make this lasagna extra decadent.
  6. Egg: An egg helps bind the ricotta so it doesn't ooze out of the lasagna when you cut into it.
